Ramen

Description

Tonkotsu ramen is a ramen dish that originated in Fukuoka , Fukuoka Prefecture on the Kyushu island of Japan, and it is a speciality dish in both Fukuoka and Kyushu. The broth for tonkotsu ramen is based upon pork bones, and tonkotsu in Japanese means "pork bones".

Ingredients

  • Ramen Noodles
  • Pork Bones
  • Chashu Pork Belly
  • Mushrooms
  • Eggs
  • Green onions
  • Soy Sauce

Instructions

  1. Boil water with pork bones for 5 hours
  2. Add mushrooms into the broth
  3. Put ramen noodles in a seperate boiling water until it is cooked
  4. Add ramen noodles to the broth
  5. Boil eggs until it is partially cooked
  6. Put eggs in broth
  7. Add green onions and soy sauce
  8. Cook chashu pork belly
  9. Add chashu pork belly into broth
  10. Serve ramen
